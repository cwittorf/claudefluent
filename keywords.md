# Keyword Strategy: Apple Jacks
**Target Persona:** Alex — 30-year-old nostalgic adult, busy lifestyle, unapologetic cereal lover. See `persona.md`.
**Goal:** Attract Alex to the site through organic and paid search. Prioritize keywords with high relevance, strong bang-for-buck (low KD + low competition + reasonable volume), and low overlap with competitor strategies.

---

## Scoring Key

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Vol** | Monthly US search volume |
| **KD** | Keyword difficulty (0–100; lower = easier to rank) |
| **Comp** | Paid competition level (LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH) |
| **CPC** | Cost-per-click estimate |
| **Intent** | commercial / informational / transactional |
| **Rating** | Bang-for-buck score: ⭐⭐⭐ = top priority, ⭐⭐ = solid, ⭐ = niche/supporting |

## What Competitors Are NOT Targeting

Based on competitive analysis (`competitors-summary.md`):

- **"cereal for dinner"** — No competitor has a page or content for this. Pure white space.
- **"are [cereal] healthy"** — Competitors avoid this question. AJ can own honest, non-defensive nutrition content.
- **"saturday morning breakfast cereal"** — All brands use nostalgia in TV/social but none have organic content built around this term.
- **"cereal ranking"** — No brand-owned ranking content in the SERP. Independent blogs own this. AJ could challenge with first-party authority.
- **"apple jacks cinnamon stick"** — 27,100 monthly searches, KD 0, no dedicated brand landing page exists.
